Program Structure
THE PPL SYSTEM

Push-Pull-Legs, 6 days a week. Each muscle group trains twice per week with adequate recovery. All working sets are 11+ reps for hypertrophy. Every session ends with a 10-min treadmill walk. Total time: under 55 minutes.

Why PPL works best: Heavy compound lifts trigger the highest anabolic hormone response of any training style. More muscle = higher resting metabolism = fat burning 24/7. The treadmill finisher keeps cardiovascular fitness improving without interfering with lifting recovery. 11+ rep targets build both strength and hypertrophy simultaneously.

MON — Push A
Chest · Triceps · Side Delts

📝 Day Notes / Reps Summary
✓ Saved
🔥Warm-up: 5 min dynamic arm swings + shoulder circles
01
Flat Barbell Bench Press
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 90 sec
Chest▼
Lie flat. Grip slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower bar to nipple line with 3 sec control, press back up. 5 points of contact: head, upper back, both feet, butt. Shoulder blades pinched down and back. Elbows 45–60° to body.
⏱ 90 sec rest between sets
02
Incline Barbell Bench Press
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 90 sec
Upper Chest▼
Bench at 30° (not 45° — too much shoulder). Bar to upper chest. Same blade positioning as flat bench. Expect to lift 10–15% less than flat.
⏱ 90 sec rest
03
Pec Deck
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Chest▼
Sit with back flat against pad. Elbows at shoulder height with slight bend. Squeeze pecs to bring arms together. Hold 1 sec at the centre. Control the return — don't let weight snap you open.
⏱ 60 sec rest
04
Overhead Tricep Rope Extension
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Triceps▼
Cable set low. Grip rope overhead, elbows close to head. Extend arms fully overhead, spreading rope slightly at the top. Lower with control — feel the long head stretch. Keep elbows fixed — only forearms move.
⏱ 60 sec rest
05
Lateral Dumbbell Raises
Target: 5 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Side Delts▼
Stand, slight forward lean. Raise arms out to sides to shoulder height — lead with elbows not wrists. Pinky slightly higher at top. Slow 3-sec descent. Keep weight light — side delts are small. No swinging.
⏱ 60 sec rest
06
Tricep Rope Pushdown
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Triceps▼
Cable set high, rope attachment. Upper arms pinned to sides. Push down until fully extended, spread rope apart at bottom. Slowly return. Slight forward lean keeps tension. Elbows must not flare.
⏱ 60 sec rest
🏃Finisher: 10 min treadmill walk (incline 3–5%)
TUE — Legs A
Quads · Hamstrings · Calves
▼
📝 Day Notes / Reps Summary
✓ Saved
🔥Warm-up: 5 min leg swings, hip circles + dynamic stretches
01
Seated Hamstring Curl
Target: 5 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Hamstrings▼
Sit upright, pad resting just above ankles. Curl legs down through full range. Squeeze at bottom. 3–4 sec eccentric (return). Toes pointed away slightly for fuller contraction. Don't let hips lift off.
⏱ 60 sec rest
02
Seated Leg Extension
Target: 5 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Quads▼
Sit with pad just above ankles. Extend legs fully, squeeze quads hard at top, lower with 3–4 sec control. Toes slightly toward you at top for fuller quad contraction. Hold 1–2 sec at top. Don't use excessive weight — this should burn, not strain.
⏱ 60 sec rest
03
Leg Press
Target: 5 × 11+ · Rest 90 sec
Quads▼
Mid foot on platform, hip-width, toes slightly out. Lower until 90° at knee, press through heels. Never lock knees at top. Don't let lower back peel off seat at bottom. Full range beats heavy partials every time.
⏱ 90 sec rest
04
Single Leg Standing Calf Raises
Target: 5 × 11+ each leg · Rest 60 sec
Calves▼
Stand on one foot on an elevated surface. Full stretch at bottom (heel below toe level), rise fully on toes, pause at top. 3 sec descent. Calves grow from full ROM and controlled tempo — not heavy partial reps. Alternate feet.
⏱ 60 sec rest
05
Sitting Calf Raises
Target: 5 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Calves (Soleus)▼
Seated calf raise machine. Knees bent at 90°. Full stretch at bottom, full rise at top. Bent knee targets soleus (deeper calf) — pairs perfectly with standing raises. Hold peak contraction 1–2 sec.
⏱ 60 sec rest
06
Seated Dumbbell Overhead Press
Target: 3 × 11+ · Rest 90 sec
Shoulders▼
Sit with back support. Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward. Press overhead until arms nearly extended. Lower with control to ear level. Core braced throughout. Don't hyperextend lower back.
⏱ 90 sec rest
🏃Finisher: 10 min treadmill walk (incline 3–5%)
WED — Pull A
Back · Rear Delts · Biceps

📝 Day Notes / Reps Summary
✓ Saved
🔥Warm-up: 5 min band pull-aparts + shoulder rotations
01
Lat Pulldown
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 90 sec
Back▼
Wide overhand grip. Lean back 10–15°. Depress shoulders first, then pull bar to upper chest, elbows driving toward floor. Full extension at top — feel the stretch. Never pull behind neck. Think "elbows to back pockets."
⏱ 90 sec rest
02
Single Hand Seated Cable Row
Target: 4 × 11+ each arm · Rest 60 sec
Back▼
Single handle attachment. Sit upright at cable row. Pull to hip/abdomen, elbow driving back. No torso rotation — isolate the back. Full stretch on return. Torso stays vertical throughout.
⏱ 60 sec rest
03
Lat Pullover
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Back▼
Lie across a bench or use cable/machine. Arms extended overhead, slight fixed elbow bend. Pull weight in an arc from overhead to hips. Squeeze lats at the bottom. Stretch fully at top every rep. Excellent for lat width.
⏱ 60 sec rest
04
Reverse Pec Deck
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Rear Delts▼
Face the pec deck, chest against pad. Elbows at shoulder height with slight bend. Pull arms back and out. Squeeze rear delts at end range. Control return — don't let it snap forward. Critical for shoulder balance and injury prevention.
⏱ 60 sec rest
05
Sitting Machine Bicep Curls
Target: 4 × 11+ · Rest 60 sec
Biceps▼
Seated preacher-style machine curl. Chest against pad. Full range: arm nearly straight at bottom, fully contracted at top. 3 sec eccentric. No momentum — the machine eliminates cheating. Most effective bicep isolation available.
⏱ 60 sec rest
🏃Finisher: 10 min treadmill walk (incline 3–5%)

Progressive Overload
PROGRESSION
SYSTEM

The 11+ rep system with double progression. Same structure for 6 months — load is what changes.

The 2-for-2 Rule: Hit your top-end reps (11+) for 2 consecutive sessions → add weight next session. Bench/Row: +2.5 kg. Leg Press/Hack Squat: +5 kg. Isolation: +1–2 kg. Never add weight if form breaks down.
▶
Double Progression — Exact Method
Applied to every lift, every session
System▼
How it works
Set a weight you can do 11 clean reps. Try to add reps each session. Once you hit 15+ reps across all sets comfortably — add weight. Reset back to 11 reps with the new weight. Repeat. This cycle drives all muscle and strength gain.
Weight Increments
  • Barbell compound lifts (bench, incline): +2.5 kg per progression
  • Leg Press: +5 kg · Hack Squat: +5 kg
  • Calf raises: +2 kg · Leg curls/extensions: +2 kg
  • Cables and dumbbells: +1–2 kg
  • Overhead press: +2 kg per progression
▶
Deload Week — Every 4 Weeks
Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24
Recovery▼
Same PPL structure. Same exercises. Drop all weights 40%. Cut 1 set per exercise. No max effort. Focus entirely on form. Treadmill walk only — no intensity. Sleep extra 30–60 min if possible. Non-negotiable — your body must have a recovery week. You always come back stronger after a deload.
The 2-for-2 Rule: Hit 11+ reps for 2 sessions in a row → add weight next session.
Deload weeks: Every 4 weeks, drop all weights 40%, reduce sets by 1. Non-negotiable.
Track everything: Log reps, sets, weight every session using the Tracker tab. No tracking = no progress visibility.
Regress when: Form breaks down, joint pain appears, or 3+ missed sessions. Drop 20% weight.
Stop immediately if: Sharp joint pain, numbness in limbs, dizziness. See a physiotherapist.
On nutrition: Eat real food — dal, rice, sabzi, eggs, curd, chicken. Protein at every meal. No liquid calories. Aim for 300–500 calorie deficit. Never go below 1700 calories. A registered dietitian will multiply your results.
Recovery System
RECOVER
OR FAIL

Lifting 6 days per week is high volume. Without systematic recovery, you'll overtrain, plateau, and get injured. This section determines results more than any exercise choice.

😴
Sleep 7–9 Hours
Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Same wake time daily, dark room, no screens 60 min before. Your most powerful anabolic tool — free and available every night.
🥩
Protein — 152g/day
1.6g per kg bodyweight minimum, spread across 4–5 meals. Eggs, paneer, chicken, dal, Greek yogurt, fish, whey as backup. Protein within 90 min post-training maximises muscle protein synthesis.
💧
Hydration 3.5–4L/day
Add 750ml per training hour. Just 2% dehydration = 10–15% strength loss. Sea salt + lemon in 1–2 glasses daily for electrolytes. Pale yellow urine = good.
🧊
Contrast Shower
Post-workout: 30s cold → 30s hot × 5 cycles, end cold. Reduces DOMS by 20–30%. Boosts dopamine 250%, norepinephrine 300%. One of the most underrated recovery tools available.
🔄
Foam Rolling 10 min/day
Priority: IT band, quads, upper back, lats, glutes, calves. 45–60 sec per area. Breathe through tender spots. Best done every morning or pre-training.
☀️
Morning Sunlight
10–20 min outdoors within 1 hr of waking (no sunglasses). Sets circadian rhythm. Critical for Indian bodies — Vitamin D deficiency is near-universal and directly suppresses testosterone and recovery capacity.
💊
Evidence-Based Supplements
Creatine monohydrate 5g/day (only supplement with consistent evidence). Vitamin D3 + K2. Magnesium glycinate (sleep and muscle recovery). Omega-3 fish oil (inflammation). Consult a doctor first.
🧘
Sunday — Full Rest
No gym. Light walk optional. High cortisol from overtraining directly blocks fat loss and muscle gain. Ideal Sunday: meal prep, extra sleep, foam rolling, and stretching. Nothing more.
